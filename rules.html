Predict Leaderboard Rules

How it works

You predict the score of every one of the 104 matches before the tournament starts. Your group-stage scores determine your group standings (FIFA tiebreakers: points, goal difference, goals scored, head-to-head). The top two from each group plus the eight best third-placed teams fill your knockout bracket exactly the way the real one is built, and you keep predicting scores all the way to the final.

For knockout matches you predict the score at the end of play (extra time included). If you predict a draw, you pick who goes through on penalties.

Scoring a match

Every match has a base value P. You have to call the result right to score anything; one tier then applies:

What you got rightPoints
Correct result — win, loss, or draw (in knockouts: the team you advanced went through)P
Correct result and correct goal difference (win/loss only)1.5×P
Correct result and exact score (win/loss/draw)2×P

Call the result wrong and you get nothing — there's no consolation for the scoreline alone. The goal-difference bonus is only available when you picked the winning team, so a predicted draw can only be upgraded by nailing the exact score.

Examples (P = 4 in the group stage):

  • You predict 2–2, it ends 3–3 → correct draw, not exact, no goal-difference bonus on draws → P (4).
  • You predict 2–2, it ends 2–2 → exact score → 2×P (8).
  • You predict 2–1, it ends 3–2 → right winner, same goal difference → 1.5×P (6).
  • You predict 2–1, it ends 1–2 → wrong winner → 0.

Knockout fine print

  • Knockout matches are scored slot-by-slot against the real bracket (match 73, match 74, …).
  • Result point (P): you get it if the team you advanced from that slot actually advanced from it — even if their real opponent wasn't the one in your bracket.
  • Goal-difference (1.5×P) and exact-score (2×P) upgrades: these only kick in when you placed both of the slot's real teams, so their predicted goals can be compared with their real goals. Get the advancer wrong and the match scores 0.
  • Penalty shootouts: a match settled on penalties is a draw in play, so there's no goal difference to earn. Predicting a draw and naming the side that went through counts like a correct goal difference → 1.5×P; nailing the exact draw score on top still earns the full 2×P.
  • Third-place teams are slotted into the bracket with the same allowed-group constraints FIFA uses. In rare edge cases FIFA's official allocation may differ from ours — the same rule applies to everyone, so it stays fair.

Submitting

  • Brackets lock at kickoff of the opening match.
  • Submit through GitHub (free account) — a robot validates your bracket instantly, and once the organizer approves it (this pool is invite-only) it's saved and shows up on the leaderboard.
  • You can resubmit as often as you like before the lock; only your latest bracket counts.
  • No GitHub account? Download your bracket file and send it to the organizer.
